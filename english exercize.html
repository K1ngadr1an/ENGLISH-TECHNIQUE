Present Perfect Simple

Form: HAVE + Past Participle

Indeterminate Past Actions

Finished actions in the past Time not specified

Examples: I have been to Spain. I have never been to Spain. I haven't been to Spain yet. Have you ever been to Spain?

Contrast: I went to Spain yesterday. Use the Past Simple for finished actions with a specific time.

Recently Finished Actions with Present Effect

Adverbs: just, already, yet

I have just eaten. I have already finished. I haven't eaten yet.

Duration

Actions started in the past that continue in the present

Examples: How long have you studied English? I've studied for 12 years. I've studied English since I was in middle school.

For: quantity of time (2 weeks, 5 minutes, etc.) Since: moment in time (six years ago, ten minutes ago, since I was a child)

Note: I played tennis for 10 years. I stopped because I broke my wrist. Here, we use the Past Simple for a completed action in the past.

Present Perfect Continuous

Focus on:

  • Effect from the action itself:
    • I'm not hungry because I've already eaten.
  • Duration of the action:
    • I'm tired because I've been shopping all day.

Examples: I've been living in Italy since 2006. They've been playing football for 16 years. But they've known each other for 2 months.

Past Perfect

Used when one action is completed before another in the past.

Examples: When I arrived home, I noticed that my wallet had been stolen. He accused John of stealing the car, but later realized that he had only forgotten where he had parked it. Before studying abroad last year, David had been studying French for seven years.
